Sovereign Smartphone

To Enjoy Freedom We Have to Control Our Phones.

Modern smartphones are complex systems where control over phone resources is exercised by phone manufacturers, operators, OS vendors, and users. Users are generally limited in what applications they can install on their devices, in the privacy model and trust assumptions of the existing applications, and in the functionalities that applications can have. Our goal is to enable users to install sensitive applications in isolated domains with protected access to selected peripherals alongside an existing OS.

Under the umbrella of the sovereign smartphone project, we explore designs that maintain compatibility with the existing smartphone ecosystem but allow the balancing of the control over the phones. Our architectures implement novel TEE-based mechanisms that allow the users to flexibly choose which resources (including peripherals) to dedicate to different isolated domains to securely execute applications.
